📚 Technical Diagnostic Guide

How to Scan USB Peripherals, HID Devices, and Hardware Capabilities Online

Modern computers connect to a wide array of peripherals, including mechanical keyboards, high-precision mice, game controllers, and custom hardware modules. If a device stops responding, profiling its raw USB or Human Interface Device (HID) descriptors is a critical troubleshooting step. Our USB & HID Device Info Profiler uses advanced WebUSB and WebHID APIs to scan peripherals directly.

ðŸ”Œ Scanning Hardware via WebUSB and WebHID APIs

Traditional websites are isolated from local hardware. However, modern WebUSB and WebHID standards enable secure, permission-granted links between browsers and peripherals. When you connect a device, our system retrieves standard product string descriptors, interface layouts, and configuration packets in real-time.

ðŸ” Decoding USB Vendor IDs (VID) and Product IDs (PID)

Every certified USB device incorporates a unique Vendor ID (VID) and Product ID (PID) assigned by the USB Implementers Forum. These 16-bit hexadecimal numbers identify the manufacturer and product model. Finding these IDs helps you locate correct device firmware updates and troubleshoot driver installation mismatches.

ðŸ›¡ï¸ Rigorous Browser Permissions and User Safety

Security is central to the WebUSB standard. Browsers prevent websites from connecting to devices automatically. A connection is only established after you explicitly click the scan button and select a device from the secure browser menu. Critical devices (like system storage) are strictly protected, ensuring absolute system safety.
