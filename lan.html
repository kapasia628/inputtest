📚 Technical Diagnostic Guide

How to Test Ethernet Latency, Network Jitter & LAN Stability Online

A stable wired internet connection is fundamental for high-performance operations, including competitive online gaming, video conferencing, and heavy cloud computing uploads. While simple speed tests evaluate bandwidth capabilities, they omit network jitter and latency variance, which are the primary causes of connection drops and packet-loss stutters.

🔌 Decoupling Jitter from Bandwidth

Jitter refers to the variation in time delay between data packets arriving over a connection. For instance, if your ping is constant at 15ms, your jitter is 0ms (perfect stability). However, if your ping bounces randomly between 10ms and 150ms, your jitter is extremely high, causing gaming lag spikes and video stream buffering even if your download bandwidth seems sufficient.

📁 Understanding Ethernet Cable Categories

The category of your Ethernet cable defines its copper shielding quality and frequency capacities. Older **Cat5** cables are limited to 100 Mbps. **Cat5e** and **Cat6** cables support Gigabit speeds up to 100 meters, while **Cat6A**, **Cat7**, and **Cat8** copper shielding reduces crosstalk noise to deliver speeds up to 40 Gbps and maintain flat, low-jitter pings.

🛠️ Diagnosing a Faulty LAN Port

If your test identifies high packet loss or random link disconnects, clean your device's RJ45 LAN port and check for bent metal contact pins inside. Outdated hardware drivers can also trigger link drops; update your motherboard's Intel or Realtek Ethernet controllers via Device Manager.
